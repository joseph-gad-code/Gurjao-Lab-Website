We study genomes to prevent and treat cancer.

Most cancers are preventable—yet less than 5% of cancer funding goes to prevention. We uncover how DNA damage arises to stop cancer before it starts. For those already facing cancer, an ever-growing number of treatments makes matching patients to the right therapy critical. By decoding each person’s genetic profile, we predict which therapies will work best – saving precious time for patients.

Our Mission

We aim to deliver data-driven precision prevention and precision treatment.
Our work broadly focuses on:

  • Why mutations occur: How lifestyle, microbes, the immune system, and DNA structure leave their mark on the genome.
  • How this knowledge guides treatment: Using tumor mutations to predict which therapies will work best.
  • Building new tools: Developing computational and experimental approaches with clinicians and physicists to push cancer research forward.
